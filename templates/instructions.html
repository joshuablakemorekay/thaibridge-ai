📖 How to use ThaiBridge AI วิธีใช้งาน

A quick guide to finding your way around. ThaiBridge AI teaches the Thai language through Thai culture and Theravada Buddhism — with an AI tutor, a dictionary, structured lessons and a meditation timer.

🚀 Getting started in 3 steps

  1. Pick a starting point. Use the Learn menu — Tones & Consonant Classes and Paiboon romanization are good places to begin.
  2. Explore a topic. Use the Learn menu for vocabulary, grammar, lessons and more — or dip into Culture and Buddhism.
  3. Ask the AI tutor. Stuck on anything? The AI Tutor can explain, give hints, and answer questions in plain English.

🔊 Learn tones early — it really matters

Thai is a tonal language: the same syllable said with a different tone becomes a completely different word (มา “come” vs หมา “dog”). If you learn tones from the very beginning, correct pronunciation becomes a habit — leave them until later and they're much harder to un-learn.

The good news: tones aren't guesswork — they follow clear rules based on consonant class. Our Tones & Consonant Classes section teaches the whole system with practice drills.

✨ What you can do

  • 🔤 Learn the script — vowels and Paiboon romanization
  • 📖 Build vocabulary — themed word sets with practice quizzes
  • 📕 Look up words — the Thai ↔ English dictionary
  • 🤖 Chat with the AI tutor — six modes of help
  • ☸️ Study Buddhism — Theravada teachings and articles
  • 🧘 Meditate — a Samatha & Vipassana timer
  • 🏆 Track progress — earn XP, level up and unlock new sections

🧡 Monk Mode (for monastics)

Monk Mode is a free track for monks and nuns — written for Thai monastics learning English and Western monastics learning Thai. When it's on, every lesson is unlocked at no cost. 🙏

  • It starts switched off. Everyone begins with Monk Mode off — it's only ever on if you turn it on yourself.
  • To turn it on, open Monk Mode in the menu and enter the access code shared by your temple or teacher.
  • To turn it off, click the "🧡 Monk Mode: ON" button in the top menu at any time — no code needed.

💡 Handy tips

  • Zoom freely. The pages adjust to your browser zoom (anywhere from 25% to 500%) — tables fill the space when there's room and scroll sideways when there isn't, so nothing gets cut off.
  • Use the side menu. On learning pages, the menu on the left stays in view as you scroll, so you can jump between topics easily.
  • Some sections are locked. Reach the required level (and, for some areas, subscribe) to open everything up.

🧭 How the pages are laid out

ThaiBridge AI doesn't force one layout on every page. Instead, each page uses the shape that fits its job — which is why a reading page looks different from a tool or a short info page. There are four:

Layout For Example pages
Landing The front door Home
3-column content Reference learning with sub-pages All the Learn pages, Culture, Buddhism
Simple centered Small info pages + short forms About, Contact, Privacy, Login, Premium
Full-width tool App-like interactive areas Dictionary, AI Tutor, Meditation, Quizzes

Matching each page to its purpose keeps things clear: you get a menu and extras where they help, and a clean, uncluttered page where they don't.
