🙏 Important Cultural Tips

The Wai (ไหว้)

The traditional Thai greeting with palms pressed together. The height of hands and depth of bow indicates respect level. Higher hands = more respect (for monks, elders, royalty).

Head & Feet

The head is the most sacred part of the body; the feet are the lowest. Never touch someone's head, point your feet at Buddha images or people, or step over someone.

Respect for Monarchy

The Thai royal family is deeply revered. Always stand for the royal anthem (played before movies), and never speak disrespectfully about the monarchy.

Temple Etiquette

Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), remove shoes before entering buildings, sit with feet tucked behind you (not pointing at Buddha images), women should never touch monks.

Saving Face (รักษาหน้า)

Thais avoid public confrontation and embarrassment. Stay calm, smile, and handle disagreements privately. Losing your temper causes everyone to lose face.

🌾 About Isan Culture

The Isan region (อีสาน) in northeastern Thailand has a distinct culture influenced by Lao language and traditions. Many forest monasteries are located here, and the Isan dialect is commonly spoken among rural communities.

Isan is known for:

  • Som Tam (ส้มตำ) - Spicy papaya salad
  • Mor Lam (หมอลำ) - Traditional music
  • Bun Bang Fai - Rocket Festival
  • Warm hospitality - "คนอีสานมีน้ำใจ"
