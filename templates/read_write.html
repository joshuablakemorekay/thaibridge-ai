How Thai letters are formed

  1. Start at the head (หัว). Almost every letter begins with a small loop or circle. Find it, and start there — it tells you where the pen goes down.
  2. One continuous stroke. Most letters are drawn without lifting the pen, in a single flowing movement out of that loop.
  3. Loop first, then the body. Draw the little circle, then carry straight on into the tall lines and curves.
  4. Left to right, top to bottom. When a letter has separate parts, work left-to-right and top-to-bottom, the same direction you read.
