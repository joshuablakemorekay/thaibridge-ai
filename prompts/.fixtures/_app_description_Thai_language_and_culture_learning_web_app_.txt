# Market Research Report — Thai Language & Culture Learning App (UK)

## Executive Summary
A clear gap exists in the UK market for a Thai learning app that integrates Buddhist culture, temple etiquette, and business language. None of the dominant platforms offer Thai at all.

## Target Market Analysis
The UK addressable market includes expats, tourists, heritage learners, Buddhists, and business professionals — over 1.4 million people with a Thailand connection. Around 1.1 million UK tourists visit Thailand each year.

## Customer Personas
Sarah, a relocating professional; David, a Buddhist practitioner; and a Thai-heritage family teaching their children.

## Competitor Analysis
The largest platforms (Duolingo, Babbel) offer no Thai course. Specialist apps lead the niche: Ling (closest competitor) and ThaiPod101 (largest content library). Neither integrates Buddhist cultural content as a core framework.

## Monetization & Pricing
A freemium model with paid subscription tiers. A free plan plus subscriptions around £7.99 and £14.99 per month, with a lifetime option. Annual plans are discounted to improve retention.

## Distribution Channels
Web-first, then mobile apps, with Buddhist community partnerships and seasonal tourism channels.

## Branding & Positioning
Mission-driven positioning around authentic Thai Buddhist culture; lotus and temple aesthetic.

## Risks & Challenges
Cultural-sensitivity risk, competition, and free-to-paid conversion are the main risks, each with mitigations.

## Go-To-Market Strategy
An 18-month phased rollout from temple partnerships to B2B corporate training.

## Feasibility
Medium-high difficulty, modest startup cost in the UK, break-even at roughly 150 subscribers, high scalability, medium overall risk. Recommendation: validate with real users before committing.
